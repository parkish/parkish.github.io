One thing that I particularly like about Sudoku is that you can solve a puzzle purely through deduction. I got a big calendar of Sudoku puzzles at one point and as I was going through them I started finding puzzles that seemed to not work that way - it appeared that the only way to solve them was to guess a value and then walk those guesses throughout the remaining squares. Which seemed really lame to me.

So to try to prove to myself that I wasn't crazy and that it was the grid's fault, NOT MINE (sorry), I wrote this little solver that runs through the techniques I use to solve Sudoku grids.

  • Grey squares mean the solver was able to deduce a value for it.
  • Squares that are unsolved/ambiguous show the remaining possibilities in red
